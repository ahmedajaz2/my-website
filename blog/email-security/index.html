Exploring how Microsoft 365, combined with SPF, DKIM, and DMARC, strengthens email trust and defends against phishing and spoofing attacks.

The Challenge

Our email environment previously faced challenges with phishing, spoofing, and unauthorized domain use. Without proper email authentication, malicious actors could impersonate our domain, eroding trust and increasing the risk of security breaches. While Microsoft Defender for Office 365 provided advanced threat protection, email spoofing at the domain layer was still a blind spot.

The Solution: Microsoft 365 & DNS Authentication in Action

1. Sender Policy Framework (SPF) - Defining Trusted Senders

Using Microsoft 365 Admin Center and our domain registrar, we implemented an SPF record in DNS specifying all authorized mail servers for our domain. We used Microsoft‚Äôs SPF record guidance (v=spf1 include:spf.protection.outlook.com -all) to cover both Exchange Online and any authorized third-party senders. This ensures receiving mail servers can verify if emails claiming to come from our domain are sent from approved IPs.

2. DomainKeys Identified Mail (DKIM) - Cryptographic Email Signing

We enabled DKIM signing in the Microsoft 365 Security & Compliance Center for our primary and secondary domains. DKIM adds a digital signature to each outgoing email, stored as a public key in DNS, allowing recipient systems to verify the message hasn‚Äôt been tampered with in transit. We generated CNAME records in DNS pointing to Microsoft‚Äôs DKIM keys (selector1._domainkey and selector2._domainkey).

3. Domain-based Message Authentication, Reporting, and Conformance (DMARC) - Enforcing and Monitoring Policy

We implemented a DMARC policy with an initial p=none setting for monitoring via aggregate and forensic reports. Over 4 weeks, we analyzed DMARC reports using Microsoft Defender for Office 365 reporting tools to identify legitimate senders not covered by SPF/DKIM. After cleaning up those gaps, we shifted to p=quarantine and finally p=reject to block unauthorized email outright.

Outcomes

  • Prevented domain spoofing by enforcing SPF, DKIM, and DMARC across all outbound mail
  • Increased trust in our email communications with customers and partners
  • Improved visibility into who is sending on behalf of our domain via DMARC reporting
  • Aligned with Zero Trust principles by ensuring authentication at every step of email delivery
